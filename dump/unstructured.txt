Quick Multiplayer Q&A
Q: How many servers are there, and where are they located?
A: We have a single server, located in US WEST.

Q: Server, shard, world... what is what?
A: Here, we user "server" to mention the physical machine hosting "shards". A "shard" is a world instance (private, public, official...). A world is a synonym for a shard.
About Shards by @mbphu 

Q: Are Multiplayer worlds consistent?
A: No. Because of how limited we are in resources, Multiplayer world will decay 5 minutes after the last player leaves.
Worlds will be persistent on release, of course.

Q: Are Multiplayer characters persistent?
A: Yes, characters are saved (they'll be wiped after Demo)

Q: How long does a Multiplayer shard last once emptied of players?
A: Once again, as it's just a Demo: a shard will survive 10 minutes of being empty. After that, the world will be deleted.

Q: How many concurrent players can there be on a single server?
A: For the Demo we've set a hard cap at 20. That being said, people will be able to tweak that cap higher at some point.

i think weâ€™ve answered most of the questions in the faq, but to reiterate - we have no plans for any sort of mtx in official shards on launch (cosmetics, stash space, experience boosts, convenience, power etc). weâ€™re ok with including cosmetic-only items in pre-launch fund raising such as the kickstarter, but it's actually something we struggle with a bit and would preferred to not have done given the choice (insomuch as we didn't see a kickstarter that didn't offer such things in return for donations as viable in any way).
we would like to create expansions which will be b2p, but this would be for major new content, in line with expansions for other games.
we do view mods as a potential avenue for generating income to support continued development and operations. other than that, we're pretty confident in our ability to optimize servers to reduce operational overhead significantly compared to other live service games. 
i think it depends on the nature of the content in the expansion. things like a new class, a new starting area, or any other content that's specifically intended for "new" characters would almost certainly require starting fresh so to speak. otoh, new world events, biomes, etc. we would want to make available to existing characters (assuming level appropriate) & worlds.
there are some major caveats to all of this though -- it's still fairly uncertain how such content would apply across a population of players that either did or did not purchase such an expansion. for example a new world event couldn't easily be restricted only to players that bought the expansion. so i think any b2p expansions would most likely contain a mix of free content (world events and the like) as well as gated content (classes and such of course, but also possibly new areas & quests if they could reasonably be segregated).
philosophically, i think it's very valuable to provide some amount of content on a regular basis anyway to all players whether or not they've bought an expansion, so long as the company itself is able to operate in a fiscally sustainable manner.
